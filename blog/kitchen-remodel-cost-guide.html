Kitchen Remodel Cost Guide: Budget to Luxury (2026)

Updated February 2026 · 10 min read

A kitchen remodel is one of the most impactful home improvements you can make, both for daily living and resale value. But kitchen renovation costs vary wildly depending on the scope, materials, and where you live. Whether you are planning a budget-friendly cosmetic refresh or a full luxury gut renovation, this guide breaks down exactly what to expect in 2026.

Quick Cost Summary by Tier

Cosmetic Refresh (paint, hardware, backsplash) $5,000 — $15,000
Minor Remodel (new counters, appliances, flooring) $15,000 — $30,000
Mid-Range Full Remodel $30,000 — $50,000
High-End Remodel $50,000 — $80,000
Luxury Gut Renovation $80,000 — $150,000+

Cost Breakdown by Component

Understanding where the money goes is key to managing your kitchen remodel budget. Here is a typical breakdown of how kitchen remodel dollars are allocated across different components and trade categories.

Cabinets & Hardware 30 — 35% of budget
Countertops 10 — 15%
Appliances 15 — 20%
Labor & Installation 20 — 25%
Flooring 7 — 10%
Plumbing & Electrical 5 — 8%
Backsplash & Tile 3 — 5%

Cabinets: The Biggest Line Item

Kitchen cabinets consistently account for the largest share of any remodel budget. Stock cabinets from big-box retailers like Home Depot or Lowe's cost $75 to $250 per linear foot and work well for budget and mid-range projects. Semi-custom cabinets run $200 to $600 per linear foot and give you more color, finish, and configuration options. Fully custom cabinetry starts at $500 per linear foot and can exceed $1,200 per linear foot for premium hardwoods with specialty finishes.

One of the most popular budget-friendly trends in 2026 is cabinet refacing, where you keep the existing cabinet boxes but replace the doors and drawer fronts. Cabinet refacing typically costs $4,000 to $10,000 for an average kitchen, compared to $12,000 to $25,000 for brand-new stock cabinets. Another money-saving option is painting existing cabinets, which runs $1,500 to $4,000 professionally and can completely transform the look of your kitchen.

Countertop Costs by Material

Your countertop choice has a massive impact on both the look and the cost of your kitchen renovation. Here is what to expect for the most popular countertop materials priced per square foot, installed.

Laminate (Formica) $15 — $40 / sq ft
Butcher Block $40 — $80 / sq ft
Quartz (Caesarstone, Silestone) $60 — $120 / sq ft
Granite $50 — $100 / sq ft
Marble $75 — $150 / sq ft
Quartzite $80 — $180 / sq ft

Quartz countertops have become the most popular choice for mid-range kitchen remodels because they require no sealing, resist stains, and come in a huge variety of colors and patterns. If you want the look of marble without the maintenance headaches, engineered quartz countertops with a marble-look pattern are an excellent choice and typically cost 30 to 40 percent less than real marble.

Appliance Costs: What to Budget

Kitchen appliance packages vary dramatically based on brand tier. A basic appliance package including refrigerator, range, dishwasher, and microwave runs $2,500 to $5,000 from brands like Whirlpool, GE, or Frigidaire. Mid-range packages from KitchenAid, Samsung, or LG typically cost $5,000 to $10,000. Premium packages from Sub-Zero, Wolf, Viking, or Thermador start at $15,000 and can exceed $30,000 for a full suite.

One area where many homeowners overspend is the refrigerator. A standard French-door refrigerator meets most families' needs for $1,500 to $3,000. Built-in panel-ready refrigerators like Sub-Zero look incredible but start at $8,000 just for the unit. Consider whether that premium is worth it for your specific renovation goals and resale timeline.

Flooring Options and Pricing

Kitchen flooring needs to be durable, water-resistant, and easy to clean. Luxury vinyl plank (LVP) has become the most popular kitchen flooring choice due to its waterproof properties, realistic wood-look finishes, and affordability at $3 to $7 per square foot installed. Porcelain tile remains a timeless option at $8 to $15 per square foot installed. Hardwood flooring costs $10 to $20 per square foot installed but requires more maintenance in a kitchen environment.

Hidden Costs Most Homeowners Miss

Kitchen remodels are notorious for going over budget. Here are the hidden costs that catch most homeowners off guard during their kitchen renovation project.

  • Permits and inspections — $200 to $2,000 depending on your municipality and the scope of work. Any electrical, plumbing, or structural changes almost always require permits.
  • Temporary kitchen setup — Most full remodels take 6 to 12 weeks. Budget $500 to $1,500 for eating out and setting up a temporary cooking area with a microwave and mini fridge.
  • Structural surprises — Opening up walls can reveal outdated wiring, asbestos, water damage, or inadequate framing. Budget an extra 10 to 20 percent as a contingency fund.
  • Demolition and disposal — Ripping out old cabinets, countertops, and flooring costs $1,000 to $4,000 for a full kitchen. Dumpster rental adds $300 to $600.
  • Lighting upgrades — Recessed lighting installation costs $150 to $300 per light. Under-cabinet LED lighting adds $200 to $500 for the whole kitchen.
  • Backsplash installation — Subway tile backsplash runs $800 to $2,500 installed. Natural stone or designer tile can cost $3,000 to $6,000.

How to Save Money on Your Kitchen Remodel

  • Keep the existing layout — Moving plumbing and gas lines is extremely expensive. If you can keep the sink, stove, and refrigerator in their current positions, you will save $3,000 to $8,000 in plumbing and electrical work alone.
  • Reface instead of replace — If your cabinet boxes are in good shape, refacing doors and adding new hardware saves 40 to 50 percent compared to new cabinets.
  • Shop appliance sales — Black Friday, Memorial Day, and Labor Day sales offer 20 to 40 percent off major appliances. End-of-model-year clearance events are also excellent for deals.
  • Use remnant stone — Countertop fabricators often have leftover granite and quartz slabs from larger jobs. Remnant pieces can save 30 to 50 percent, especially for smaller kitchens or islands.
  • DIY the demolition — Removing old cabinets, flooring, and backsplash yourself saves $1,000 to $3,000 in labor costs.
  • Get at least three contractor quotes — Pricing varies significantly between contractors in the same area. Get three to five detailed quotes and compare line by line.

Kitchen Remodel Timeline: What to Expect

A cosmetic kitchen refresh with new paint, hardware, and backsplash can be completed in 1 to 2 weeks. A minor remodel with new countertops, appliances, and flooring takes 3 to 6 weeks. A mid-range full kitchen renovation runs 8 to 12 weeks. A major gut renovation with layout changes and structural work can take 12 to 20 weeks, especially if permits and inspections are required.

Planning and material lead times add to the total timeline. Custom cabinets take 6 to 12 weeks to manufacture. Specialty countertop materials may have 2 to 4 week lead times. Appliance backorders are less common in 2026 than they were during the pandemic years, but premium brands can still have 3 to 6 week waits.

Does a Kitchen Remodel Increase Home Value?

A kitchen remodel is one of the highest ROI home improvement projects. According to national averages, a minor kitchen remodel recoups 75 to 80 percent of its cost at resale, while a major mid-range remodel returns 50 to 60 percent. The key is proportionality — your kitchen renovation budget should not exceed 5 to 15 percent of your home's total value to maximize return on investment.

In competitive housing markets, an updated kitchen can be the deciding factor between offers. Buyers consistently rank kitchens as the most important room when evaluating homes, and a dated kitchen is often the primary reason buyers negotiate lower prices or pass on a property entirely.

When to Hire a Designer vs. Going Solo

For cosmetic updates and minor remodels, most homeowners can handle the planning themselves using free design tools from IKEA, Home Depot, or Lowe's. For mid-range and high-end remodels, a professional kitchen designer adds $2,000 to $8,000 to the budget but can save you from costly layout mistakes, help you maximize storage, and ensure your material selections work cohesively. If your remodel involves moving walls, plumbing, or electrical, hiring a designer or architect is strongly recommended.

Red Flags When Hiring a Kitchen Contractor

  • Asking for more than 30 percent upfront — reputable contractors typically ask for 10 to 15 percent as a deposit
  • No written contract with detailed scope of work, materials list, and payment schedule
  • No proof of license, insurance, or bond in your state
  • Pressuring you to start immediately without a design plan
  • Unusually low bid — this often means cut corners, unlicensed subcontractors, or bait-and-switch pricing
  • No references or portfolio of completed kitchen projects
  • Unwilling to pull permits for electrical or plumbing work

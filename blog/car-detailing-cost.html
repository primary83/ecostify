How Much Does Car Detailing Cost in 2026?

Updated February 2026 · 11 min read

A professional car detail does more than make your vehicle look good. It preserves your paint, protects your interior from UV damage and stains, and can add hundreds of dollars to your resale value. But detailing prices vary wildly depending on what you get, who does it, and how large your vehicle is. Understanding the pricing tiers before you book ensures you get exactly the service you need without overpaying.

In 2026, car detailing typically costs between $50 and $300 for most vehicles, with a standard full detail running $150 to $250 at a reputable shop. Premium or show-quality details can reach $500 or more, while basic wash-and-vacuum packages start as low as $30. Below, we break down every service tier, vehicle size adjustment, and cost factor so you can book with confidence.

Quick Cost Summary

Here is what you can expect to pay for the most common detailing services in 2026. Prices reflect mid-size sedan rates at an average independent detailer.

Basic exterior wash and dry $25 – $50
Interior detail only (vacuum, wipe, glass) $75 – $150
Exterior detail only (wash, clay, wax) $80 – $160
Full detail (interior + exterior) $150 – $300
Premium detail with paint correction $300 – $600+

SUVs, trucks, and minivans typically add 20 to 40 percent to these prices due to the larger surface area and interior space. A full detail on a Ford Expedition will cost noticeably more than the same service on a Honda Civic.

Detailing Service Tiers Explained

Detailing is not a one-size-fits-all service. Most detailers offer tiered packages that correspond to different levels of work, products, and time invested.

Basic wash and vacuum is the entry-level service. It typically includes an exterior hand wash, tire dressing, window cleaning, interior vacuuming, and a dashboard wipe-down. This takes 30 to 60 minutes and is suitable for regular maintenance between full details. It will not address embedded dirt, stains, or paint imperfections, but it keeps your car presentable.

Interior detail goes significantly deeper than a basic vacuum. A thorough interior detail includes steam cleaning or shampooing carpets and cloth seats, conditioning leather surfaces, cleaning and dressing all plastic and vinyl trim, detailed cleaning of air vents, cup holders, and door jambs, and interior glass cleaning. Pet hair removal, odor elimination, and stain extraction are often available as add-ons. A proper interior detail takes two to four hours depending on the vehicle's condition.

Exterior detail focuses on restoring and protecting your paint. It starts with a thorough wash, followed by a clay bar treatment to remove bonded surface contaminants like tree sap, industrial fallout, and road tar. After claying, the detailer applies a polish or sealant and finishes with a coat of wax or spray sealant. Tire dressing, wheel cleaning, and trim restoration are included. An exterior detail takes two to three hours.

Full detail combines interior and exterior services into a comprehensive package. This is the most popular option and delivers the best overall value since bundled pricing is lower than booking interior and exterior separately. A full detail takes four to six hours for a mid-size sedan in average condition.

Premium or show-quality detail adds paint correction (machine polishing to remove swirl marks, scratches, and oxidation), multi-step paint protection, engine bay cleaning, and meticulous attention to every surface. This level of service is appropriate for enthusiasts, pre-sale preparation, or vehicles that have never been professionally detailed. Premium details can take a full day or longer.

Vehicle Size and Condition Pricing

Your vehicle's size and current condition are the two biggest variables after the service tier you choose.

Compact car (Civic, Corolla) — full detail $130 – $220
Mid-size sedan (Camry, Accord) — full detail $150 – $275
SUV / Crossover (RAV4, Highlander) — full detail $180 – $350
Full-size truck (F-150, Silverado) — full detail $200 – $375
Minivan (Sienna, Odyssey) — full detail $200 – $400

Minivans often cost the most to detail because of their expansive interior surfaces, multiple seating rows, and the likelihood of heavy use with children and pets. A minivan interior with ground-in Cheerios, juice stains, and dog hair takes significantly more time and effort than a lightly used sedan.

Vehicle condition also impacts price. Most detailers quote based on a vehicle in average condition. If your car has not been detailed in years, has excessive pet hair, heavy staining, or significant paint contamination, expect a surcharge of $50 to $150 for the extra time and products required.

Mobile Detailing vs. Shop Detailing

You have two main options for where the work happens, and each has distinct advantages.

Shop-based detailers operate from a fixed location with dedicated bays, professional lighting, and access to full equipment. They tend to deliver more consistent results because they control the environment. Many shop detailers specialize in paint correction and ceramic coating application, which require controlled conditions to do properly. Shop detailing is generally the best choice for premium services.

Mobile detailers come to your home or office, which eliminates the need to drop off your car and wait. Mobile detailing has exploded in popularity in recent years, with services accessible through apps and online booking. Prices are often comparable to shop rates, and some mobile detailers charge slightly less due to lower overhead costs. The main limitation is that mobile detailers may not be able to perform paint correction or apply certain coatings that require a dust-free environment. For standard interior and exterior details, mobile service is extremely convenient and delivers excellent results.

For most drivers, mobile detailing is the better choice for routine maintenance details, while shop-based detailing is preferable for paint correction, ceramic coating application, or show-quality work.

Common Add-On Services and Costs

Many detailers offer add-on services that target specific problems or provide extra protection.

Pet hair removal $30 – $75
Odor elimination (ozone treatment) $50 – $150
Headlight restoration (pair) $40 – $100
Engine bay cleaning $30 – $80
Fabric/leather protection coating $40 – $100
Paint sealant upgrade (6-month protection) $50 – $120

Pet hair removal is one of the most common add-ons and one of the most labor-intensive. Removing embedded pet hair from cloth seats and carpet requires specialized tools and significant time. If you regularly transport pets, this add-on is well worth the extra cost.

Odor elimination using ozone treatment is highly effective for smoke smells, mildew, and other persistent odors that cannot be removed by cleaning alone. The ozone generator runs inside the sealed vehicle for several hours, breaking down odor-causing molecules at the chemical level.

How Often Should You Detail Your Car?

The ideal detailing frequency depends on how you use your vehicle, where you park, and how much you care about maintaining its appearance and value.

For most drivers, a full detail every four to six months keeps the vehicle in excellent condition. Between full details, a basic wash every two to four weeks prevents contaminant buildup and makes each detail session easier and faster.

If you park outdoors, drive in dusty or polluted areas, have pets, or eat in your car frequently, you may benefit from detailing every three months. Vehicles that are garaged, driven sparingly, or already protected with ceramic coating can often go six to twelve months between full details.

Regular detailing is not just cosmetic. It protects your paint from corrosion, prevents interior materials from cracking or fading, and maintains your vehicle's resale value. A well-maintained car with documented detailing history can command $500 to $2,000 more at sale compared to a neglected vehicle of the same age and mileage.

Red Flags When Choosing a Detailer

The detailing industry has a low barrier to entry, which means quality varies widely. Watch for these warning signs.

Prices that seem too low. If a full detail is advertised for $50 on a sedan, corners are being cut. Quality products, proper tools, and the time required for thorough work have real costs. An unrealistically cheap detail usually means a superficial cleaning with inferior products that can actually damage your vehicle's surfaces.

No portfolio or reviews. Reputable detailers maintain an online presence with photos of their work and customer reviews. If you cannot find any evidence of their work quality, proceed with caution.

Rushing through the job. A proper full detail takes four to six hours for a mid-size sedan. If a detailer claims to finish a full detail in under two hours, they are not performing a thorough service. Speed is the enemy of quality in detailing.

Using automatic brushes or harsh chemicals. Professional detailers hand-wash vehicles and use pH-balanced products designed for automotive finishes. Brush washes and household cleaning products cause swirl marks and can strip wax, sealant, and even clear coat over time.

Money-Saving Tips for Car Detailing

You can get professional results without breaking the bank with these strategies.

Book a membership or package deal. Many detailers offer monthly memberships or multi-visit packages at a 15 to 25 percent discount compared to one-time pricing. If you plan to detail regularly, a package is the best way to save.

Maintain between details. Keeping your car clean between professional details reduces the work required each time, which keeps costs down. A weekly rinse, interior trash removal, and quick vacuum take 15 minutes and make a noticeable difference.

Skip add-ons you do not need. Not every car needs engine bay cleaning or headlight restoration at every visit. Focus your budget on the core interior and exterior detail, and add extras only when genuinely needed.

Consider doing the interior yourself. A decent vacuum, microfiber towels, and an all-purpose interior cleaner can handle basic interior maintenance for under $30 in supplies. Save the professional interior detail for quarterly deep cleans and handle the in-between maintenance yourself.

Invest in paint protection. A ceramic coating or quality sealant makes your car easier to clean and maintain, reducing the frequency and cost of professional detailing over time. The upfront cost pays for itself within a year or two of reduced detailing expenses. For additional exterior enhancements, explore options like car wraps or window tinting.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between a car wash and a car detail? A car wash cleans the surface. A car detail cleans, corrects, and protects every surface inside and out. Detailing includes processes like clay bar treatment, polishing, and conditioning that a car wash never touches. A detail takes hours, while a wash takes minutes.

Is car detailing worth it? Yes, for most vehicle owners. Regular detailing protects your investment, maintains resale value, and keeps your vehicle looking and smelling its best. The cost of detailing is modest compared to the depreciation you avoid by keeping your car in excellent condition.

How long does a full detail take? A standard full detail takes four to six hours for a mid-size sedan in average condition. Larger vehicles, heavily soiled interiors, or premium services with paint correction can take eight hours or more. Plan to drop off your car for most of the day or book a mobile detailer who works while you are at home or the office.

Should I tip my detailer? Tipping is not required but is appreciated, especially for mobile detailers who bring their own equipment and water. A tip of 15 to 20 percent for excellent work is customary and consistent with other personal services.

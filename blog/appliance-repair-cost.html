When a major household appliance stops working, the first question most homeowners ask is straightforward: how much will the repair cost? The answer depends on the appliance type, the specific problem, your location, and whether you need parts replaced. In 2026, the average appliance repair cost falls between $150 and $600, though some jobs can run higher if a compressor, motor, or control board needs replacing.

This guide breaks down the appliance repair cost for washers, dryers, refrigerators, dishwashers, and ovens. We cover common repairs, service call fees, when to repair versus replace, and practical ways to save money on every service visit.

Quick Cost Summary

Washing machine repair$150 – $500
Dryer repair$100 – $450
Refrigerator repair$200 – $600
Dishwasher repair$150 – $500
Oven / range repair$150 – $600
Service call / diagnostic fee$75 – $150

Most technicians charge a flat diagnostic fee that gets rolled into the total if you proceed with the repair. Parts and labor together make up the rest of the bill. Below, we look at each appliance in detail.

Cost by Appliance Type

Washing Machine Repair Cost: $150 – $500

Washing machines take a beating. Between heavy loads, hard water deposits, and constant vibration, components wear out over time. The most common washing machine repair cost drivers include pump replacements, belt swaps, lid switch fixes, and control board issues.

Pump replacement$200 – $400
Belt replacement$120 – $180
Lid switch / door latch$100 – $200
Control board$250 – $500
Water inlet valve$120 – $225

Front-load washers tend to cost more to repair than top-loaders because their parts are harder to access and the components themselves are more expensive. If your washer is leaking, failing to drain, or making grinding noises during the spin cycle, a technician can usually diagnose the problem in under an hour.

Dryer Repair Cost: $100 – $450

Dryers are mechanically simpler than washers, which often keeps repair bills lower. The most frequent dryer repairs involve heating elements, thermal fuses, drum belts, and rollers.

Heating element$150 – $300
Thermal fuse$100 – $200
Drum belt$120 – $200
Drum rollers / bearings$150 – $250
Timer / control board$200 – $400

Gas dryers typically cost $50 to $100 more to repair than electric models because technicians need to handle gas valve coils and igniters. If your dryer runs but produces no heat, the heating element or thermal fuse is the most likely culprit and one of the more affordable fixes.

Refrigerator Repair Cost: $200 – $600

Refrigerators are the most expensive common appliance to repair because they rely on sealed refrigerant systems and complex electronic controls. The refrigerator repair cost climbs quickly when compressor or sealed-system work is needed.

Thermostat replacement$150 – $300
Evaporator fan motor$200 – $350
Compressor replacement$400 – $800
Defrost timer / heater$175 – $350
Ice maker repair$150 – $350

Compressor replacements are the big-ticket item. If your fridge is over ten years old and the compressor fails, replacement usually is not cost-effective. For smaller issues like a faulty defrost timer or a noisy fan motor, repair is almost always the smarter financial move.

Dishwasher Repair Cost: $150 – $500

Dishwashers have a shorter average lifespan than other major appliances—roughly 9 to 12 years—so the repair-vs.-replace calculus matters more here. Common dishwasher repairs include pump replacements, spray arm fixes, door latch issues, and control panel problems.

Pump motor$200 – $400
Door latch / gasket$100 – $200
Spray arm replacement$100 – $175
Control panel / board$200 – $450
Water inlet valve$120 – $225

If your dishwasher is leaving dishes dirty, not draining, or leaking from the door, these are among the more affordable repairs. A dishwasher that will not start at all usually points to a control board or door latch issue, which sits in the mid-range of repair costs.

Oven & Range Repair Cost: $150 – $600

Oven repairs vary widely depending on whether you have a gas or electric model and whether the issue is with the cooktop, the oven cavity, or the electronic controls.

Heating element (electric)$150 – $300
Igniter (gas)$175 – $350
Thermostat$150 – $300
Door hinge / gasket$100 – $250
Control board$250 – $600

Gas oven igniter replacement is one of the most common calls technicians handle. If the oven takes a long time to preheat or does not ignite at all, the igniter is typically to blame. Electric oven elements are also straightforward to replace and sit at the lower end of the price spectrum.

Common Repairs Across All Appliances

Regardless of the appliance, certain repair categories come up again and again. Understanding these helps you evaluate a technician's quote:

  • Control boards: Modern appliances are computer-controlled. Board replacements run $200 to $600 depending on the model and are among the priciest single-component fixes.
  • Motors and pumps: Drain pumps, circulation pumps, fan motors, and drum motors cost $150 to $400 to replace, including labor.
  • Heating elements: Found in dryers, ovens, dishwashers, and some washers. Replacements usually fall between $120 and $350.
  • Seals, gaskets, and latches: Door gaskets, tub seals, and latch assemblies are affordable at $75 to $200 and often prevent leaks and operational errors.
  • Valves: Water inlet valves, gas valves, and solenoids run $100 to $250 installed.

Repair vs. Replace Guide

Deciding whether to repair or replace an appliance comes down to age, repair cost, and expected remaining lifespan. A commonly used guideline is the 50-percent rule: if the repair costs more than half the price of a new equivalent appliance, replacement usually makes more sense.

Here are average lifespans for reference:

Washing machine10 – 14 years
Dryer10 – 13 years
Refrigerator12 – 18 years
Dishwasher9 – 12 years
Oven / range13 – 20 years

If an appliance is within the first half of its expected life, repair is almost always worthwhile. In the final quarter of its lifespan, lean toward replacement—especially if the repair involves a major component like a compressor or motor.

Service Call Fees

Most appliance repair companies charge a service call fee of $75 to $150 just to show up and diagnose the problem. This covers the technician's travel time and the first 15 to 30 minutes of diagnostic work. Many companies waive this fee or apply it as a credit toward the repair if you go ahead with the work.

Some companies offer flat-rate pricing that bundles the diagnostic and repair into one quote, while others charge separately for labor and parts. Always ask about the fee structure before booking so there are no surprises when the bill arrives.

Factors That Affect Appliance Repair Cost

Several variables push the final price up or down:

  • Appliance brand: Premium brands like Sub-Zero, Viking, and Miele use proprietary parts that cost significantly more than standard components from Whirlpool, GE, or Samsung.
  • Part availability: If the part needs to be special-ordered, you may wait days and pay a premium for shipping. Older models sometimes require discontinued parts that are only available through salvage.
  • Location: Urban areas with more competition tend to have lower labor rates, while rural or remote locations may include higher travel charges.
  • Warranty status: Appliances under manufacturer warranty or an extended protection plan may have repairs covered entirely. Always check before paying out of pocket.
  • Emergency and weekend service: After-hours, weekend, and holiday calls often carry a 25 to 50 percent surcharge over standard rates.
  • Complexity of access: Built-in refrigerators, stacked washer-dryer units, and appliances in tight spaces take longer to service, increasing labor charges.

Red Flags When Hiring a Repair Technician

Protect yourself from overcharges and subpar work by watching for these warning signs:

  • No written estimate: A reputable technician will always provide a written quote before starting work. Walk away from anyone who insists on working without one.
  • Pressure to replace immediately: Some technicians push full replacements because the commission is higher. Get a second opinion if a repair quote seems unreasonably high.
  • No license or insurance: In many states, appliance repair technicians need a license. Always verify credentials and ask for proof of liability insurance.
  • Unusually low quotes: A quote that is dramatically cheaper than others may signal the use of aftermarket parts of questionable quality, or hidden fees that appear after work begins.
  • No warranty on labor: Professional repair companies typically guarantee their work for 30 to 90 days. Avoid anyone who will not stand behind the repair.

Money-Saving Tips

Appliance repairs are rarely planned expenses, but there are ways to keep the cost manageable:

  • Get multiple quotes: Three estimates give you a reliable range. Most companies provide free or low-cost phone estimates for common issues.
  • Check your home warranty: If you have a home warranty plan, appliance repairs may be covered for just a service call copay of $75 to $125.
  • Ask about the diagnostic fee: Many companies waive the service call fee when you approve the repair. Confirm this policy before scheduling.
  • Consider aftermarket parts: OEM parts are not always necessary. For older appliances, quality aftermarket components can cut parts costs by 30 to 50 percent.
  • Perform basic maintenance: Clean dryer lint traps and vents regularly, replace refrigerator water filters on schedule, and run dishwasher cleaner monthly. Prevention avoids many common failures.
  • Tackle simple fixes yourself: Replacing a dryer belt, swapping a dishwasher door gasket, or changing an oven igniter are beginner-friendly repairs with plenty of video tutorials available.
  • Bundle repairs: If multiple appliances need service, some companies offer discounts for handling everything in one visit.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a typical appliance repair cost in 2026?

Most appliance repairs cost between $150 and $600 including parts and labor. Simple fixes like a thermal fuse or door latch sit at the lower end, while compressor or control board replacements reach the upper end. The national average across all appliance types is roughly $300.

Is it worth repairing a 10-year-old refrigerator?

It depends on the repair. A $200 thermostat replacement on a 10-year-old fridge with a 15-year expected lifespan is a smart investment. A $700 compressor replacement on the same unit is borderline—you may be better off putting that money toward a new, more energy-efficient model.

How long does an appliance repair take?

Most repairs are completed in one to two hours once the technician arrives with the correct parts. If parts need to be ordered, a follow-up visit is usually scheduled within three to five business days.

Do I need to be home for the repair?

Yes. Someone over 18 typically needs to be present to authorize the work and provide access. Some companies offer scheduled time windows to minimize waiting around.

Can I repair the appliance myself?

Some repairs are straightforward DIY projects, including belt replacements, gasket swaps, and filter changes. However, any work involving gas lines, sealed refrigerant systems, or high-voltage components should be left to licensed professionals for safety reasons.
